# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## Do's

1. **Keep your profiles professional** – Use a clear 
   name and appropriate profile picture on platforms 
   like LinkedIn and GitHub.

2. **Think before you post** – Always consider whether 
   your post could affect your academic or professional 
   reputation before publishing.

3. **Use privacy settings** – Regularly review and 
   update privacy settings on all social media accounts.

4. **Share your achievements** – Post certifications, 
   projects, and milestones on LinkedIn to build 
   your professional presence.

5. **Report harmful content** – If you encounter 
   cyberbullying, hate speech, or fraud, report it 
   using the platform's reporting tools.

## Don'ts

1. **Don't share personal information publicly** – 
   Avoid posting your phone number, address, or 
   college ID on any public platform.

2. **Don't engage in online arguments** – Responding 
   to negative or provocative comments can damage 
   your reputation and mental health.

3. **Don't post confidential academic content** – 
   Sharing exam papers, answer keys, or internal 
   college documents online is unethical and may 
   have consequences.

4. **Don't click on suspicious links** – Avoid 
   clicking unknown links sent via DMs or comments, 
   even from people you know.

5. **Don't plagiarize or misrepresent yourself** – 
   Never claim skills, certifications, or experiences 
   you do not have on professional platforms.
